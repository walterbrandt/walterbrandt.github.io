Looking back at the Chicago Bears disappointing 2024 season

bears
chicago
nfl
Author

Walter Brandt

Published

April 14, 2025

The Chicago Bears went into the 2024 season with high hopes after drafting Caleb Williams with the first overall pick in last year’s draft. Fans would be let down after the Bears went on to go 5-12, finishing in last place in the NFC North. It is not all doom and gloom for Chicago, they did bad enough to get yet another top ten draft pick in this year’s draft.

Code 
library(tidyverse)

    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10),

There was a lot of hype going into the 2024 season with what analysts said was one of the best wide receiver cores in the league with DJ Moore, Rome Odunze and Keenan Allen who the Bears traded for from the Chargers in the offseason. Although there were some flashes of promise last season it is clear that the Bears struggled heavily with their rookie quarterback and abysmal offensive line. The Bears finished amongst the worst teams in the NFL this year in terms of points for.

The Bears also claimed De’andre Swift in free agency which had players and fans alike excited about a dual threat offense but there was not much of a threat, especially in the run game. The Bears were second to last in broken tackles with only 12. Chicago was also second to last place in yards after carry per attempt. It doesn’t take a football guru to know that you need a good run game to open up the pass game, not being able to break tackles and not running for many yards gives way for longer second downs, making the game much harder for the offense.

It’s expected for a rookie quarterback to struggle in their first season. However Caleb Williams took a beating his first year, leading the league in sacks. Although he led the league in by a decent margin other quarterbacks got sacked a lot as well. However, what is different for Williams is the amount of yards he gave up by getting sacked.

Code

When most people think about the Bears they think of the Monsters of the Midway and hard-nosed defense. However, in reality the Bears were really not that great on defense either. One thing to look at is their quarterback hurries and hurry percent. They rank somewhere in the bottom middle meaning that they give opposing quarterbacks too much time to make a decision leading to big plays.
