A look into the Chicago Bears 2024 struggles

Author

The Chicago Bears went into the 2024 season with high hopes after drafting Caleb Williams with the first overall pick in last year’s draft. Fans would be let down after the Bears went on to go 5-12, finishing in last place in the NFC North. It is not all doom and gloom for Chicago, they did bad enough to get yet another top ten draft pick in this year’s draft.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(ggalt)
library(ggtext)
library(gt)
library(ggrepel)
offstats <- read_csv("nfl24.csv")

bears <- offstats |>
  filter(
    Team == "Chicago Bears"
  ) 
ggplot() + 
  geom_bar(data=offstats, aes(x=reorder(Team, PF), weight=PF), fill="lightgrey") + 
  geom_bar(data=bears, aes(x=reorder(Team, PF), weight=PF), fill="orange") +
  coord_flip() + 
  labs(
    x="", 
    y="Points For", 
    title="Chicago's offensive struggles", 
    subtitle="The Bears are at the bottom of the barrel for NFL offense", 
    caption="Source: Pro Football Reference | By Walter Brandt"
    ) +
  theme_minimal() +
 theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 20, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 10), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
    )

There was a lot of hype going into the 2024 season with what analysts said was one of the best wide receiver cores in the league with DJ Moore, Rome Odunze and Keenan Allen from the Chargers in the offseason. Although there were some flashes of promise last season it is clear that the Bears struggled heavily with their rookie quarterback and abysmal offensive line. The Bears finished amongst the worst teams in the NFL this year in terms of points for.
